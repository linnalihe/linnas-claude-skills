# Scoring Framework for Conversion Copy

## Value Proposition Clarity (0-10)

### 10/10 - Exceptional
- Benefit is stated in first 10 words
- Outcome is specific and measurable (e.g., "increase revenue by 34%")
- Customer instantly understands "what's in it for me"
- Zero jargon or technical language
- Directly addresses customer's desired transformation

### 7-9/10 - Strong
- Benefit is clear within first paragraph
- Outcome is stated but could be more specific
- Customer-focused but might have minor clarity issues
- Minimal jargon that doesn't impede understanding
- Good connection to customer goals

### 4-6/10 - Needs Improvement
- Benefit takes too long to find
- Outcomes are vague (e.g., "improve your business")
- Mix of features and benefits, not clear which is which
- Some jargon that creates friction
- Somewhat customer-focused but inconsistent

### 1-3/10 - Poor
- No clear benefit stated
- Focus on features rather than outcomes
- Company-focused instead of customer-focused
- Heavy jargon and technical language
- Unclear what customer actually gets

### 0/10 - Conversion Killer
- Impossible to determine what the product does
- Entirely feature-focused
- No mention of customer outcomes
- Confusing or contradictory messaging

---

## Emotional Engagement (0-10)

### 10/10 - Exceptional
- Opens with a pain point customer viscerally feels
- Creates strong desire for transformation
- Uses sensory language and specific scenarios
- Builds emotional urgency (fear of missing out, relief from pain)
- Reader can see themselves in the story

### 7-9/10 - Strong
- Addresses pain points clearly
- Creates moderate desire
- Some emotional resonance
- Includes scenario customer relates to
- Good emotional hooks

### 4-6/10 - Needs Improvement
- Pain points mentioned but not felt
- Weak desire creation
- Mostly logical, limited emotional appeal
- Generic scenarios that don't resonate strongly
- Missed opportunities for emotional connection

### 1-3/10 - Poor
- No pain point addressed
- No emotional engagement
- Entirely rational/logical approach
- No storytelling or scenarios
- Reads like a spec sheet

### 0/10 - Conversion Killer
- Actively creates negative emotions (confusion, frustration)
- No connection to customer experience
- Cold, corporate tone that repels

---

## Call-to-Action Strength (0-10)

### 10/10 - Exceptional
- Single, crystal-clear action
- Creates urgency with specifics (time-limited, scarcity, FOMO)
- Removes all friction (no credit card, instant access, etc.)
- Action-oriented verb (Start, Get, Join, etc.)
- Includes social proof at point of action ("Join 10,000 users")
- Low commitment language ("Try", "Start free", etc.)

### 7-9/10 - Strong
- Clear single action
- Some urgency created
- Low friction
- Good action verb
- Might be missing one optimization element

### 4-6/10 - Needs Improvement
- Multiple CTAs that compete
- Weak or no urgency
- Some friction present
- Weak verb ("Click here", "Learn more")
- Missing social proof or risk reversal

### 1-3/10 - Poor
- Unclear what to do next
- No urgency
- High friction (complex signup, payment upfront)
- Passive language
- Multiple competing actions

### 0/10 - Conversion Killer
- No CTA present
- Completely unclear what customer should do
- Maximum friction

---

## Overall Conversion Potential (0-10)

Holistic assessment combining all factors:

- **9-10**: Will drive significant revenue, ready to deploy
- **7-8**: Strong foundation, minor optimizations needed
- **5-6**: Has potential but needs material improvements before launch
- **3-4**: Major revision required, would lose significant revenue as-is
- **0-2**: Complete rewrite necessary, will fail to convert
