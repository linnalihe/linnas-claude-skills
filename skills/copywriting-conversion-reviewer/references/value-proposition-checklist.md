# Value Proposition Clarity Checklist

## The 5-Second Test

A strong value proposition should pass this test:
- A stranger reads it for 5 seconds
- They can tell you exactly what you sell
- They can tell you who it's for
- They can tell you why it matters to them

2. **Where will I be?** (Desired future state)
3. **How do I get there?** (The bridgeâ€”your product)

**Example:**
- Now: "Drowning in spreadsheets, can't see which marketing works"

### âœ… Benefit Hierarchy

Structure your copy in this order:
1. **Ultimate benefit** (the transformation)
2. **Primary benefits** (top 3 outcomes)
3. **Features** (only as proof of benefits)

**Example:**
1. Ultimate: "Run your entire marketing from one dashboard"
2. Primary: "Save 10 hrs/week | Make data-driven decisions | Prove ROI to your boss"
3. Features: "Connects to Google Ads, Facebook, LinkedIn..."

### âœ… The "So What?" Test

For every claim, ask "So what?"

**Weak:**
- "Our platform has AI-powered analytics"
- So what?
- (No answer = conversion killer)

**Strong:**
- "Our platform has AI-powered analytics"
- So what?
- "So you get automatic insights instead of staring at charts"
- So what?
- "So you make confident budget decisions in minutes, not hours"

### âœ… No Jargon or Buzzwords

**Avoid:**
- Leverage
- Synergy
- Innovative
- Next-generation
- Revolutionary
- Disruptive
- Best-in-class
- Industry-leading
- Cutting-edge
- Solutions
- Ecosystem

**Use Instead:**
- Simple, clear language a 12-year-old could understand
- Specific verbs: save, increase, eliminate, get, start
- Concrete nouns: time, money, customers, revenue

## Quick Audit Questions

1. Can a stranger understand what this is in 10 words or less?
2. Is the benefit in the headline/subject line?
3. Are outcomes specific and measurable?
4. Would a customer say "that's exactly what I need"?
5. Is it clear what they'll be able to do after buying?

If you answer "no" to any of these, rewrite immediately.
